# American Express — Blue Cash Everyday Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: American Express Blue Cash Everyday Card
- codename: The Grocery Grinder
- issuer: American Express

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: $200 cash back after spending $2,000 in purchases in the first 6 months
- intro_apr: not relevant to this player (always pays in full); 0% intro APR period exists on purchases but carries no value here

## Rewards
- 3% cash back at U.S. supermarkets, on up to $6,000/year in purchases (1% after cap)
- 3% cash back on U.S. online retail purchases, on up to $6,000/year in purchases (1% after cap)
- 3% cash back at U.S. gas stations, on up to $6,000/year in purchases (1% after cap) — not relevant to this player, who doesn't drive
- 1% cash back on everything else

## Strengths (argue these)
- Best-in-class verified reward rates for this player's exact top categories: 3% on groceries AND 3% on online shopping, both areas the rubric weights heavily (20 + 10 points)
- $200 welcome bonus is fully verified and a realistic target ($2,000/6 months is achievable even at this player's low <$300/mo spend if combined with normal living costs)
- $0 annual fee, satisfying the no_annual_fee criterion outright
- Amex reports to all three credit bureaus, supporting the builds_credit goal

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- Approval odds for a player with zero U.S. credit history are genuinely uncertain: the Amex/Nova Credit "Credit Passport" program that once let newcomers from India use their foreign credit file to apply for U.S. Amex cards appears to have been discontinued in 2025 (the official Amex newcomers page now 404s). Without that pathway, this card likely requires Amex's standard underwriting, which typically wants some history — this is the single biggest risk for this player
- The 3% gas-station category (one-third of the bonus structure) is dead weight for this player, who has no car and rarely uses gas
- $6,000/year category caps are irrelevant at this player's spend volume (a non-issue, but also means the "high cap" marketing doesn't add real value here)
