# Chime — Chime Card (Credit Builder Secured Visa)

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: Chime Card (formerly Chime Credit Builder Secured Visa Credit Card; rebranded 2026 with cash back tiers)
- codename: The Open Door
- issuer: Chime (issued via Stride Bank / The Bancorp Bank, secured Visa)

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: none (verified — no traditional sign-up bonus exists for this card)
- intro_apr: no interest is ever charged on this card (it's a secured spend-what-you-load model), so APR is a non-factor either way for this player

## Rewards
- Chime Plus members: 2% cash back on a category of choice
- Chime Prime members: up to 5% cash back on a category of choice
- Cash back is deposited into the cardholder's Secured Deposit Account roughly 9 days after the purchase settles
- Membership tiers (Plus/Prime) are qualified via account activity (e.g., direct deposits), not a paid subscription fee

## Strengths (argue these)
- Verified no credit check required for approval at all — the single strongest approval-odds case in this entire roster for a player with zero U.S. credit history, directly maximizing the heaviest-weighted approval criterion alongside the welcome bonus
- $0 annual fee and literally no interest charged, ever
- Up to 5% cash back (Prime tier) on a category the player picks — selecting groceries or bills means this card can out-earn every other no-fee card in this roster on the player's top category
- Reports to all three major credit bureaus, directly serving the builds_credit goal even for an applicant with no prior file

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No welcome bonus at all — a real loss on the rubric's most heavily weighted criterion (25 points)
- Requires opening a Chime Checking Account and self-funding a secured deposit to set the spending limit — this is a bank-account commitment beyond "just get a credit card," more friction than a standalone card application
- The 5% rate requires Prime-tier qualifying activity (e.g., a set level of direct deposits); a player without steady U.S. income/payroll may only reach the lower 2% Plus tier
- As a secured, deposit-funded card rather than a revolving credit line in the traditional sense, it builds credit more slowly/differently than a standard unsecured card with a real credit limit
