# Capital One — Savor Student Cash Rewards Credit Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: Capital One Savor Student Cash Rewards Credit Card
- codename: The Grocery Gladiator
- issuer: Capital One

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: $100 cash bonus after spending $300 on purchases within the first 3 months from account opening (limited-time offer)
- intro_apr: None listed (no 0% intro APR; standard variable APR applies from day one)

## Rewards
- 3% cash back at grocery stores (excludes superstores like Walmart and Target)
- 3% cash back on dining
- 3% cash back on entertainment
- 3% cash back on popular streaming services
- 1% cash back on all other purchases
- No foreign transaction fees

## Strengths (argue these)
- Highest verified grocery rate in the student card field at 3%, directly targeting the player's largest spend category (groceries weighted 25 in rubric)
- $100 welcome bonus with a $300 spend threshold is realistic for a student — achievable in the first month of groceries and bills alone
- Capital One explicitly accepts students with limited or no US credit history, making approval odds strong (approval_odds_no_us_history weighted 15 in rubric)
- 3% on dining also covers the player's secondary spend category; the 1% floor applies cleanly to bill payments with no wasted category penalty

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No elevated rate for online shopping — bills and general online spend earn only 1%, capping value in those categories
- Grocery exclusion for Walmart and Target may catch the player off-guard if they shop at superstores
- APR range of 18.49%–28.49% variable is mid-range; carrying a balance is expensive
