# Capital One — Quicksilver Secured Cash Rewards Credit Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: Capital One Quicksilver Secured Cash Rewards Credit Card
- codename: The Sure Thing
- issuer: Capital One

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: none (verified — this secured card carries no sign-up bonus, unlike its unsecured Quicksilver sibling)
- intro_apr: not relevant to this player (always pays in full)

## Rewards
- Unlimited 1.5% cash back on every purchase, every category, no caps
- 5% cash back on hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel (not relevant to this player)

## Strengths (argue these)
- Among the most reliably approvable real cash-back cards for someone with zero U.S. credit history: no minimum credit score, accepts thin/no-file applicants, and a refundable deposit starting at just $200 — this directly serves the heaviest-weighted criterion (approval_odds_no_us_history, 15 pts) with near-certainty rather than hope
- Reports to all three credit bureaus, squarely serving the builds_credit goal
- $0 annual fee
- Flat 1.5% on everything means zero wasted categories — every dollar of this player's grocery, bill-pay, dining, and online spend earns the same straightforward rate, with nothing wasted on gas or drugstores

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No welcome bonus at all (verified) — this is a real cost against the 25-point upfront_welcome_bonus criterion, the single heaviest-weighted item in the rubric
- Requires a security deposit (starting at $200), which is an upfront cash outlay other cards in this roster don't require
- 1.5% flat is mediocre on groceries specifically compared to cards offering 2-3% in that category — this card wins on certainty, not on category rate
