# Chase — Freedom Rise Credit Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: Chase Freedom Rise Credit Card
- codename: The Slow Climber
- issuer: Chase (JPMorgan Chase Bank)

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: $25 statement credit after enrolling in autopay within the first 3 months of account opening
- intro_apr: none

## Rewards
- 1.5% cash back on all purchases (base rate, no bonus categories)
- 3% cash back on dining for the first 6 months (limited-time promotional offer, up to $6,000 in purchases; active as of June 2026)
- 2% cash back on Lyft rides through September 30, 2027
- Note: no elevated grocery or online shopping rewards

## Strengths (argue these)
- Flat 1.5% on all purchases — including utility bills and online shopping — provides a consistent floor rate that beats the 1% base of competing cards (bills_floor_rate weighted 10 in rubric)
- Available to applicants with limited or no credit history; approval odds improve significantly if the applicant holds a Chase checking or savings account with at least $250
- Chase is one of the largest US issuers — approval here opens doors to the broader Chase ecosystem (Freedom Flex, Sapphire) as credit history develops
- No annual fee ensures zero cost of ownership

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No elevated grocery rewards at all — the player's single heaviest spend category (groceries weighted 25) earns only 1.5%, which every competitor matches or beats
- Welcome bonus is a meager $25 autopay credit — effectively no upfront bonus (upfront_welcome_bonus weighted 20; this card scores near zero)
- Charges a 3% foreign transaction fee, which can sting an international student making cross-border payments or sending money home
- Approval without an existing Chase account is less certain; the player has no US banking relationship established
