# U.S. Bank — Cash+ Secured Visa Credit Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: U.S. Bank Cash+ Secured Visa Credit Card
- codename: The Category Customizer
- issuer: U.S. Bank

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: none (verified — no sign-up bonus exists for this secured card on U.S. Bank's official page or any third-party source checked)
- intro_apr: not relevant to this player (always pays in full)

## Rewards
- 5% cash back on up to $2,000 in combined quarterly purchases across two categories of the cardholder's choice (categories include things like TV/internet/streaming, home utilities, cell phone providers, electronics stores, gyms, fast food, department stores, furniture stores, and movie theaters — note: grocery stores are NOT one of the 5% options, verified)
- 2% cash back on one "everyday" category of choice: gas stations, grocery stores, OR restaurants (only one of the three can be selected) — choosing groceries here gets 2% on this player's largest recurring category
- 1% cash back on all other purchases
- Security deposit required, $300 to $5,000, refundable

## Strengths (argue these)
- "Home utilities" is selectable as a 5% category, directly serving this player's bills_floor_rate criterion at a rate (5%) far above a typical floor rate
- $0 annual fee
- Reports to credit bureaus and offers a path to graduate to the unsecured Cash+ Visa Signature Card with the deposit returned, serving the builds_credit goal
- Flexible category selection means the player can tune the card to avoid dead categories like gas or drugstores entirely

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No welcome bonus at all (verified) — a clear loss on the rubric's heaviest-weighted criterion
- Groceries can only be selected at 2%, and only by giving up the gas/restaurant 2% slot — and groceries cannot be put in the stronger 5% tier at all, so this card structurally underperforms on the groceries_rewards criterion compared to competitors offering 2-3% on groceries without trade-offs
- Requires a security deposit (minimum $300, higher than several competitors' $200 minimums)
