# Bank of America — Customized Cash Rewards (Student)

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: BofA Customized Cash Rewards for Students
- codename: The Strategist
- issuer: Bank of America

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: $200 after $1,000 spend in 90 days
- intro_apr: 0% for 15 billing cycles (purchases + balance transfers within 60 days)

## Rewards
- 2% automatic at grocery stores & wholesale clubs
- 3% on ONE choice category you pick (online shopping, dining, gas/EV, travel, drugstores, or home improvement)
- 1% on everything else
- cap: 3%/2% rates apply to first $2,500 combined per quarter, then 1%
- note: choice category changeable once per calendar month

## Strengths (argue these)
- Only card here with a real upfront bonus AND category coverage on both groceries and online shopping.
- You control the 3% category, so zero reward is wasted on gas/drugstores you never use.
- $200 bonus clears fast for a groceries+bills spender.

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- Approval is the hardest of the field with no US credit history; BofA is the strictest issuer.
- $2,500 combined quarterly cap limits high earners.
- Dining only earns the base rate unless chosen as the category.
