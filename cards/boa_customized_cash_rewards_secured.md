# Bank of America — Customized Cash Rewards Secured Credit Card

> Contestant dossier. An agent reads this file and argues the case for THIS card.

## Identity
- name: Bank of America Customized Cash Rewards Secured Credit Card
- codename: The Builder's Pick
- issuer: Bank of America

## Offer
- annual_fee: $0
- welcome_bonus: none (verified — Bank of America's own product page states secured cards are not eligible for the new-account cash bonus that the unsecured sibling card offers)
- intro_apr: not relevant to this player (always pays in full)

## Rewards
- 3% cash back in a category of the cardholder's choice (gas, online shopping, dining, travel, drug stores, or home improvement/furnishings), plus an extra 3% bonus in year one (6% combined first year, 3% after) — choosing "online shopping" directly serves this player's online_shopping_rewards criterion
- 2% cash back at grocery stores and wholesale clubs
- 1% cash back on everything else
- 2%/3% category rewards apply up to a combined $2,500 in quarterly purchases (then 1%) — far above this player's spend volume, so the cap is never a binding constraint

## Strengths (argue these)
- Lets the player choose "online shopping" as the 3%/6% category while still getting 2% on groceries automatically — a genuinely good two-category fit for this player's actual spend (groceries + online shopping are explicitly weighted 20 + 10 points)
- $0 annual fee
- Designed specifically for building/rebuilding credit; reports to the bureaus, and the security deposit is eligible for return after a future account review once a credit history is established
- No exposure to gas/drugstore/home-improvement waste as long as the player picks "online shopping" as their bonus category

## Weaknesses (the rival will attack these)
- No welcome bonus at all (verified, contradicting widely-repeated marketing about a $200 bonus, which applies only to the unsecured version) — a real loss against the heaviest-weighted criterion in the rubric
- Requires a security deposit to open the account
- The 6%-in-year-one structure is really "3% base + 3% first-year bonus" — after year one it drops to a more modest 3%, and the player must actively select and possibly switch their bonus category online to keep optimizing
